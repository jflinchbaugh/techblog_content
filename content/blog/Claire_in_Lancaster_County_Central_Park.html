title=Claire in Lancaster County Central Park date=2009-08-26 05:21:18.864 type=post status=published tags=Photography ~~~~~~

The Lancaster Camera Club hosted an outdoor portrait event at Lancaster County Central Park in the Garden of the Five Senses, so I (easily) convinced Claire to get all dolled up and come out to be my model.

10s of people showed up for the event of all levels of experience, and after a very quick introduction, they all set out shooting with the various models that came out. I figured that I had my model, my lights, and camera, so Claire and I stepped aside, found our initial setting, and started shooting.

Our starting spot was hidden from others by some bushes, so I setup my light stand and new softbox out in the open, so people could see I was down there as we were getting started. One person joined us, I dialed in the lights for her, and she got shooting a little bit observing how the strobe worked balanced with the ambient evening light. Claire did a great job working with other photographers.

We continued a slow lap around the gardens, mostly by ourselves, getting shots along various paths, then caught up with the group. One of the club leaders, Doug, was demonstrating using a big gold reflector with some models on the little waterfall fountains in the middle of the garden.

We played a bit there, watched others for a bit, then wandered back out into the open to shoot some more.

Through the night, I'd jump in occasionally and try to grab a few shots with other models in other people's setups, but I wasn't really trying, and I didn't have the necessary control, so I didn't have anything worth keeping other than my shots with Claire. Those came out wonderfully.

I shot mostly with the 50mm f/1.8, and maybe a little with the 28mm f/2.8. Once I started using the new 28" softbox, I didn't really stop -- it's nice and light and easy to carry on the stand. I'd occasionally pop out the silver reflector whe I needed that extra fill light from the bottom of the image.

I can't wait to get out again with Claire to shoot -- just from the couple images we got, I have more ideas. It was a great location, but I can do alright in most locations, I think, especially if I can shoot Claire.

All the images are in the gallery.
