title=Camping at Lake Fairfax Park date=2009-07-06 08:13:56.014 type=post status=published tags=Family, Photography ~~~~~~

Over Father's Day weekend, we went camping at Lake Fairfax Park in Reston, VA. It was a great trip, and we found lots to do even in a bit of rain.

The park itself was nice and clean, and it had a water park -- Paige and Ben were pretty independent floating around the various pools and toys in their little life vests. I even got in the water to play a bit and to float around the cool lazy river.

We didn't stay at the campground long, though, since the place is located conveniently near the Udvar Hazy Air and Space Museum on the edge of the Dulles Airport, and we were about 20 minutes from a Metro stop. We also found restaurants and other things we needed within a few miles of the park.

Udvar Hazy is amazing, since it's huge! There's an SR-71, a Concorde, and even an entire Space Shuttle. We picked a good day to be there, since it was their Family Become a Pilot day or something, so the place had lots of activities for the kids. It's also indoors, so that was useful in Saturday morning's thunderstorms.

On Sunday, we grabbed breakfast and hopped the Metro down to the Smithsonian Museums. We checked out primarily the Museum of American History, then walked the mall down to the new World War 2 Memorial and to the Lincoln Memorial.

Paige was quite excited about the Lincoln Memorial, since she had learned about Lincoln in school. I think I'm supposed to send a copy of the photo of her at the monument to her Kindergarten teacher or something.

Everyone got to play in the water, see lots of stuff, and I came back with a photo or two, so the trip was a great success.
